Dantini

   Deriving his moniker by merging the names of his two favorite magicians, Dante and Houdini, he was an eccentric prestidigitator and violinist who often preformed in a tuxedo and sneakers. Dantini discovered magic at an early age, leaving home in his mid-teens to preform vaudeville in Philadelphia and then to a sideshow on Coney Island.

   He returned to Baltimore in his early twenties, sustaining himself with various job; first a freak show on "the block" and then with mentalist Rajah Raboid. For years, at precisely ten o' clock pm... seven days a week and mignight on the weekends, Dantini performed his famous 15 minute routine at the Peabody Book Store.

   It was said he approached each performance as if playing The Palace in New York. One night at the Peabody Book Store, as it neared ten o' clock Dantini told the owner "I must go on... NOW!." After the act, Dantini sat down and lost consciousness. He died a few days later on March 14th 1979 at Mercy Hospital, fondly remembered by all.
