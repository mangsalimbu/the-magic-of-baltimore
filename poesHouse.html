Edgar Allen Poe's house in Baltimore

   On September 27, Poe left Richmond for New York. He went to Philadelphia and stayed with a friend named James P. Moss. On September 30, he meant to go to New York but supposedly took the wrong train to Baltimore. Poe then stayed and lived in Baltimore with his cousin, Virginia, and his aunt, Maria, from 1832 to 1835.

   While living in Baltimore, he wrote one of his first horror stories, "Berenice," as well as "MS Found in a Bottle," which he submitted to the Baltimore Saturday Visitor, winning a $50 prize. He spent most of his time here at 203 Amity Street
