Don Driver

   Born July 25th 1943 in Rosedale, Md., Don caught the magic bug early in his life, his interest stoked further after attneding a filming of Phil Thomas's Sunday morning "Magic Man" program...Don Spent three years in the Navy where in his spare time he studied the craft, afterwards landing a slot as a magician for Harry Beck's Circus-O-Rama.

   Inspired by George Goebel, the early seventies found Driver had perfected his straight jacket escape from a lifetime study of his hero, Houdini. In the mid-seventies, Don left Baltimore for a life of thrills and danger, working more side shows.

   Over the years, Don honed his pitchman skills working with legends like Bobby Reynolds and John Bradshaw. Don Driver has spent his life living it to the fullest with a series of fun-filled ventures, gaining wisdom and incite the into the human condition, and garnering praise from his peers.

   Don Driver... magician, escape artist, sideshow preformer, pitchman, raconteur, and teacher.
